Your ideal savings

Don't buy so many avocado toasts, good amount of savings can save you when things get tough. How much should you save each month? Let's find out.


The 10 - 20 - 30 - 40 rule

10%

The short-term reserve to cover the unpredictable like a broken washing machine, fridge or car. Best is to keep this part on your savings account so you can get to it quickly when needed, but at the same time it isn't so tempting and easy to spend it on other things.

20%

Dedicate one fifth of your income to the "worse times". You can put it into building savings, retirement savings or invest into mutual funds. This reserve can finance the studies of your children, a reconstruction or it can help to keep a good standard of living after you retire.

30%

This part represents the costs for housing. Approximately one third of your income should go for the bills for water, gas, electricity, rent, mortgage etc. It can be very difficult to achieve this, since the prices for rent and housing in general increased rapidly over past few years.

40%

Max. forty percent of your income should be spent on food, transportation, school, hobbies, phones, vacations, clothing etc.
